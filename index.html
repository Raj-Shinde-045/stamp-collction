History of Indian Stamps

Indian stamps have a rich history that dates back to the 19th century. The first stamp issued in India was the "Scinde Dawk" in 1852, which was a circular design in red, white, and blue. This was followed by the famous "Four Annas" stamp in 1854, which was the first multicolored stamp in the world.

Over the years, Indian stamps have depicted a wide range of themes, including historical events, cultural heritage, and notable personalities. The stamps have also evolved in terms of design and printing techniques, reflecting the advancements in technology and the changing tastes of collectors.

Today, Indian stamps are highly sought after by philatelists around the world, and they continue to be a popular way to commemorate important events and milestones in the country's history.
